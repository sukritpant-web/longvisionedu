TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language)

TOEFL is a standardized test designed to measure the English language proficiency of non-native speakers. It is widely accepted by universities, colleges, and institutions worldwide.

Types of TOEFL Tests

  • TOEFL iBT (Internet-Based Test): The most common and widely accepted version.
  • TOEFL iBT Home Edition: Taken at home, monitored by a live human proctor.
  • TOEFL ITP (Institutional Testing Program): Used for placement and progress evaluation.
  • TOEFL Junior: For students aged 11 and above.
  • TOEFL Primary: For younger students aged 8-11.

TOEFL iBT Test Structure

The TOEFL iBT test consists of four sections:

Section Duration Questions/Tasks Score Range
Reading35 minutes20 questions0-30

Speaking16 minutes4 tasks0-30

Total test duration: Approximately 2 hours
Total score range: 0-120

Scoring System

  • Each section is scored on a scale of 0-30, and these scores are combined for a total score ranging from 0-120.
  • The scores are typically valid for two years.

Test Administration

  • The TOEFL iBT is offered at authorized test centers worldwide.
  • The Home Edition allows test-takers to take the exam from home under online proctoring.
  • Results are typically available within 6-10 days after the test date.

Key Features

  • Measures academic English skills as used in university settings.
  • Integrates multiple language skills to reflect real-life academic tasks.
  • Provides enhanced score reporting with personalized feedback and performance insights.
  • Widely accepted by over 11,500 institutions in more than 160 countries.
TOEFL remains a crucial exam for non-native English speakers aiming to study or work in English-speaking environments, providing a comprehensive assessment of their language abilities in academic contexts.
