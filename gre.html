GRE (Graduate Record Examination)

The GRE is a standardized test widely used for admission to graduate and business schools worldwide. It assesses verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, and analytical writing skills.

Test Structure and Content

The GRE consists of three main sections:

Section Time Questions Score Range
Verbal Reasoning60 minutes40 questions130-170
Quantitative Reasoning70 minutes40 questions130-170
Analytical Writing60 minutes2 essays0-6

Total test time: Approximately 3 hours and 45 minutes (including breaks)

Scoring

  • Verbal and Quantitative sections: 130-170 (1-point increments)
  • Analytical Writing: 0-6 (0.5-point increments)
  • Scores are valid for five years.

Key Features

  • Computer-adaptive test for Verbal and Quantitative sections.
  • Offered year-round at test centers worldwide.
  • An at-home testing option is available.
  • Allows test-takers to skip questions and return to them later.
  • Free ScoreSelectÂ® option to choose which scores to send to schools.

Why Take the GRE?

  • Widely accepted for various graduate programs.
  • Required for many PhD programs.
  • Accepted by some business schools as an alternative to GMAT.
  • Used for awarding fellowships and assistantships.

Test Preparation

  • Use official ETS preparation materials.
  • Take practice tests to familiarize yourself with the format and timing.
  • Focus on improving vocabulary and math skills.
  • Develop strategies for each question type.

Global Acceptance

The GRE is accepted by thousands of institutions in numerous countries, including:

  • United States
  • Canada
  • United Kingdom
  • Australia
  • Germany
  • Netherlands
  • Singapore
The GRE provides a comprehensive assessment of skills crucial for success in graduate-level studies, making it a valuable tool for both applicants and admissions committees in evaluating readiness for advanced education.
